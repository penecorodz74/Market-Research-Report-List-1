<p><p>Vacuum measuring instruments are categorized into three main types based on the level of vacuum they measure. Low vacuum instruments operate in the range of atmospheric pressure to 1 mbar, commonly used in applications like packaging and drying. Medium vacuum instruments function between 1 mbar and 0.001 mbar, ideal for processes such as coatings and material research. High vacuum instruments measure pressures below 0.001 mbar, essential for applications like semiconductor manufacturing and scientific research, where precise vacuum conditions are critical.</p></p>
