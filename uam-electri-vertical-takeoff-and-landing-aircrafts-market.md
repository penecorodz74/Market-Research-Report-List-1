<p><p>The Urban Air Mobility (UAM) market for Electric Vertical Takeoff and Landing (eVTOL) aircraft is divided into two main propulsion types: vector propulsion and vectorless propulsion. Vector propulsion systems, like thrust vectoring, enable more maneuverability and efficiency by directing the thrust direction during flight. In contrast, vectorless propulsion relies on fixed thrust outputs, simplifying design and operation but potentially limiting agility. Both types cater to urban transportation needs, enhancing future mobility solutions while addressing concerns like noise and energy efficiency.</p></p>
