<p><strong>The Soluble Dietary Fiber Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Inulin</li><li>Polydextrose</li><li>Pectin</li><li>Beta-glucan</li><li>Others</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The soluble dietary fiber market encompasses various types, including inulin, polydextrose, pectin, and beta-glucan, each offering unique health benefits and functional properties. Inulin, derived from plants, aids in digestion and enhances gut health. Polydextrose serves as a low-calorie sweetener and bulking agent. Pectin, commonly found in fruits, acts as a gelling agent and supports heart health. Beta-glucan, primarily sourced from oats and barley, is known for its cholesterol-lowering effects. Other types of soluble fibers include psyllium and guar gum, contributing to diverse applications in food products.</p></p>
