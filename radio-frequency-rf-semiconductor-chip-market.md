<p><p>The radio frequency (RF) semiconductor chip market is categorized into high-frequency and low-frequency segments. High-frequency RF chips operate typically above 1 GHz and are essential for applications like 5G communication, radar systems, and satellite transmissions, enabling faster data rates and improved signal integrity. In contrast, low-frequency RF chips function below 1 GHz, often used in devices such as RFID systems, automotive keyless entry, and industrial communications, focusing on longer range and lower power consumption. Both segments are crucial for advancing wireless technology.</p></p>
