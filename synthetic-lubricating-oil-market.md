<p><p>The synthetic lubricating oil market comprises various types, including organic esters, synthetic hydrocarbons, polyether, polysiloxane (silicone oil), containing oil, and phosphate-based lubricants. Organic esters offer excellent lubricating properties and biodegradability, while synthetic hydrocarbons are known for their stability and performance at high temperatures. Polyether lubricants provide low-temperature fluidity, and polysiloxane is favored for its thermal stability and resistance to oxidation. Containing oils blend multiple bases for tailored performance, whereas phosphate-based oils enhance anti-wear and extreme pressure properties, making them suitable for specialized applications.</p></p>
