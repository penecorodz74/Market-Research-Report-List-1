<p><p>Cranial fixation and stabilization systems are crucial in neurosurgery for securing cranial components post-surgery. The market is divided into two primary types: resorbable fixation systems and nonresorbable fixation systems. Resorbable systems gradually dissolve in the body, eliminating the need for removal surgery and minimizing long-term complications. In contrast, nonresorbable systems provide lasting stability but require subsequent surgical intervention for removal. Each type offers unique advantages, influencing surgeons' choices based on patient needs and clinical scenarios.</p></p>
