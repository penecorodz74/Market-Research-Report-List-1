<p><p>Electrode binders are crucial components in lithium-ion batteries, ensuring the adhesion of active materials to current collectors. Anode binders are typically used in the negative electrode, enhancing the stability and conductivity of materials like graphite. In contrast, cathode binders are utilized for the positive electrode, supporting materials such as lithium cobalt oxide. The choice of binder affects the battery's overall performance, longevity, and energy efficiency, making them vital for the development of advanced battery technologies.</p></p>
