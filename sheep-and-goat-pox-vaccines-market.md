Killed vaccines, on the other hand, contain inactivated pathogens, making them safer with a reduced risk of disease transmission. They typically require booster doses to maintain immunity but can be preferable for certain at-risk or sensitive animals. Both types are essential for disease management in livestock.</p></p>
