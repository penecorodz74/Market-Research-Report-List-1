<p><p>IO expander chips enhance the number of input/output pins on microcontrollers. They come in various types: </p><p>1. **8-Bit Expanders**: Suitable for basic applications, these provide a small number of additional GPIOs for simple tasks.</p><p>2. **16-Bit Expanders**: Offering a balance between performance and complexity, they cater to moderate applications needing more I/O.</p><p>3. **32-Bit Expanders**: Designed for advanced applications, they support extensive control and monitoring with a high pin count.</p><p>4. **Other Types**: Include specialized expanders for specific communication protocols or features, addressing unique project requirements.</p></p>
