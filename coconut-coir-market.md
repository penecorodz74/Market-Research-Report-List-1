<p><p>Coconut coir is categorized into three main market types: brown fiber, white fiber, and others. Brown fiber is derived from mature coconuts, known for its durability and strength, making it ideal for brushes, mattresses, and erosion control mats. White fiber comes from younger coconuts and is softer and finer, primarily used in horticulture, upholstery, and textiles. The "others" segment includes various coir products like coconut coir pots and geotextiles, catering to diverse applications across industries.</p></p>
